## 6. Why the partnership makes sense (and where it doesn't)

**Honest read first:** Docthub's audience is overwhelmingly Indian healthcare professionals working in India. There is no direct overlap with Halcyon's US-practice ICP. A "promote Halcyon to Docthub's doctors" pitch will not convert.

**Three angles where the partnership has substance:**

### Track A — India Hiring Channel (immediate cash, no-regrets leg)
Halcyon (like most US RCM firms) runs offshore coding / billing / AR-calling teams. Docthub Jobs is a credible, healthcare-specific recruiting channel inside India. Halcyon onboards as a paid recruiter, runs a co-branded "RCM careers" microsite, hires 5 FTEs in 90 days through Docthub Jobs. Cash to Docthub: standard recruiter fees + a Halcyon retainer.

### Track B — Recruiter Sub-Channel (recurring annuity)
Docthub claims **9,000+ healthcare recruiters**. Many are connected to Indian-American physician owners (~125K Indian-origin physicians in US). Halcyon's 25%/10% economics motivate recruiters to sub-promote. Docthub gets a **20% override** on every closed practice. Recurring, no marginal cost.

### Track C — Diaspora Association Introductions (strategic upside)
Docthub serves Indian medical associations (membership-management product). Several Indian-American physician associations (AAPI chapters, specialty societies) have parallel structure. Docthub's intro letter to AAPI/IMG associations is a low-cost, high-fit warm intro for Halcyon. Direct commission to Docthub on closed practices.

**Where it does NOT work:**
- Selling Halcyon directly to Docthub's user base of Indian-resident doctors. They are not US providers.
- Treating Docthub as a US-doctor media channel. It is not one.

**Bottom line for the proposal:** lead with **Track A** as the immediate-value reciprocal hook. Layer **Track B** and **Track C** as the scaling motions. Position Halcyon's 25%/10% as the structural mechanism that lets Docthub stand up its first-ever third-party partner program without building it from scratch.
